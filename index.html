About Me

Scott Kasper [skot kas-per] noun. 1. Loving father and husband. 2. Demonstrated leader in EMS and Healthcare. 3. Avid cyclist. 4. Advocate and volunteer

I am a 32-year Emergency Medical Services veteran and a proven leader in EMS and Healthcare Administration. My strengths in strategic planning, operations, process improvement, revenue cycle management and more, have allowed me to build and maintain high-performing systems that provide excellent clinical services in a fiscally successful manner. Throughout my career I have collaborated with State, County and local government officials, and diverse stakeholder groups working diligently to effect passage of updated laws, and innovative regulations. I have translated my leadership skills into the hospital and other areas of healthcare, working collaboratively with physicians, nurses, and program administrators to improve care across the continuum.

I am a devoted husband and father of three amazing sons. My true passion is enjoying active outdoor activities with each of them. I am an outspoken advocate, educator and fundraiser for JDRF (Juvenile Diabetes Research Foundation). I have served in leadership roles including Chapter Board President and member of the JDRF National Speaker’s Bureau. In 2012 I became a USA Cycling certified Coach, and work on a national level with the JDRF Ride to Cure Program combining my love of cycling with my passion to find a cure for Type 1 Diabetes.
